"Our Business Is Too Unique for Automation"
We hear this a lot. And it's usually not true. What's actually true is that your business has unique processes, but those processes are typically built from standard components. "We've Tried Automation Before and It Didn't Work"
This is more common than you'd think. Usually, the issue wasn't automation itself—it was the approach. Previous attempts often failed because they tried to automate everything at once, or they automated the wrong processes first, or they didn't account for the change management side of implementation. We take a different approach: start with the highest-leverage processes, implement in phases, and build in time for your team to adapt. This dramatically increases success rates.
"Implementation Will Disrupt Our Operations"
This is a legitimate concern. But here's what we've learned: the disruption from poor implementation is real, but the disruption from NOT automating is often worse. You're already losing productivity, making errors, and missing opportunities every day. Automation disruption is typically a 2-4 week adjustment period, after which you're operating at a higher level.
"We Don't Have Budget for This Right Now"
Understandable. But consider this: if your ROI is 300%, then automation is essentially paying for itself in 4 months. After that, it's pure profit. Most companies find that the ROI is so strong that they can fund automation from the savings it generates.
"What If It Doesn't Deliver the Promised ROI?"
Fair question. This is why we structure engagements with clear success metrics and regular check-ins. We track actual vs. projected ROI at 30, 60, and 90 days. If we're not on track, we adjust the approach. We don't get paid until you're seeing results.
